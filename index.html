About Deen_Guide

What this web app does

Deen_Guide is an Islamic companion built to make daily learning and worship easier. It brings Quran reading, hadith browsing, prayer times, dhikr counting, Islamic questions, bookmarks, and quiz learning into one simple place.

Our goal

We want this to become more than a website. With your support and feedback, we would like to develop Deen_Guide into a full mobile app that is fast, helpful, and easy for Muslims to use every day.

Built for benefit

The project is designed for students, families, and anyone who wants a gentle way to read, remember, revise, and grow in Islamic knowledge.

Services

Quran Tools

Read Quran translations, view Arabic verses, listen to reciters, save bookmarks, and continue from your last read surah.

Daily Worship

Check prayer times, use the digital tasbih, track dhikr, and keep useful Islamic reminders close at hand.

Learning Features

Study hadith, ask the Islamic assistant common questions, play the quiz game, and explore prophet stories in the History page.

Future App Plans

We hope to add mobile app support, offline access, better learning paths, more stories, reminders, and improved community feedback features.

History & Stories of Prophets

Short reminders from the lives of noble prophets, written for reflection and learning.

Prophet Adam (AS)

Adam (AS) was the first human being and the first prophet. His story reminds us that people can make mistakes, return to Allah sincerely, and be forgiven through repentance.

Allah honored Adam (AS) with knowledge and taught him names. His story also teaches that arrogance is dangerous, because Iblis refused Allah's command out of pride.

Prophet Nuh (AS)

Nuh (AS) called his people to worship Allah for many years with patience and courage. His story teaches persistence, trust in Allah, and remaining firm even when few people listen.

He built the ark by Allah's command while people mocked him. The believers were saved, showing that success is with obedience to Allah, not with popularity.

Prophet Ibrahim (AS)

Ibrahim (AS) stood against idol worship and submitted fully to Allah. He is remembered for deep faith, sacrifice, hospitality, and complete trust in his Lord.

He questioned false worship with wisdom and was protected by Allah when thrown into fire. His family also became connected to the building of the Kaaba and the rites of Hajj.

Prophet Ismail (AS)

Ismail (AS) is remembered for obedience, patience, and trust in Allah alongside his father Ibrahim (AS).

His story teaches surrender to Allah's command and respect between parent and child. He is also connected with Makkah, Zamzam, and the building of the Kaaba.

Prophet Yusuf (AS)

Yusuf (AS) faced jealousy, separation, temptation, prison, and hardship, yet remained patient and faithful.

Allah raised him in honor after years of difficulty. His story teaches chastity, forgiveness, good character, and trusting Allah's plan even when life feels hidden from us.

Prophet Ayyub (AS)

Ayyub (AS) is remembered for patience during severe illness and loss, while continuing to rely on Allah.

He made dua with humility, and Allah relieved his hardship. His story comforts anyone facing trials and reminds believers that patience with Allah is never wasted.

Prophet Yunus (AS)

Yunus (AS) called his people to Allah and is known for his dua from the darkness inside the fish.

His supplication, "La ilaha illa Anta, subhanaka, inni kuntu minaz-zalimin," is a powerful reminder of repentance, humility, and Allah's mercy.

Prophet Musa (AS)

Musa (AS) faced Pharaoh with the message of truth and relied on Allah through hardship. His story shows bravery, leadership, and the power of Allah over oppression.

Allah supported Musa (AS) with signs and opened a path through the sea. His story teaches courage when speaking truth and patience with difficult people.

Prophet Dawud (AS)

Dawud (AS) was given wisdom, leadership, and a beautiful recitation of the Zabur.

His story reminds us that strength should be joined with worship, justice, and humility. He was a king and a servant of Allah.

Prophet Sulayman (AS)

Sulayman (AS) was given a great kingdom and special abilities by Allah, yet he recognized all blessings as gifts from his Lord.

His story teaches gratitude, wise leadership, and using power responsibly. He asked Allah for a kingdom and remained devoted to worship.

Prophet Isa (AS)

Isa (AS) was given miracles by Allah and called people to worship Allah alone. His story reminds us of mercy, humility, and devotion to the message of tawheed.

By Allah's permission, he healed the sick and gave hope to people. Muslims honor him as a mighty messenger and believe he was not divine, but a servant and prophet of Allah.

Prophet Muhammad (SAW)

Muhammad (SAW) is the final prophet, sent as a mercy to mankind. His life teaches patience, honesty, kindness, justice, worship, and excellent character.

He transformed people through Quran, mercy, and beautiful manners. His Sunnah remains a guide for worship, family life, community, leadership, and personal character.
